If you rent it out — two things to do (not in the math)

Serve the AB 1482 written exemption notice. A single-family home is exempt from the statewide rent cap (Costa-Hawkins) — but only if you serve the required written notice. Without it the unit can fall under the cap (5% + CPI). The model assumes market-rate rent growth, which presumes the exemption is preserved.
Plan to sell vacant — and know the model can't price two things. An SF single-family rental is subject to just-cause eviction; a tenanted home is harder to sell with vacant possession and tends to fetch less. The realistic plan is to time a sale around a tenant move-out and sell the house empty, which sidesteps most of that — but it does mean your exit isn't fully on your schedule. Two costs of holding the model leaves out (no honest dollar figure exists for either): (1) that reduced timing flexibility, and (2) concentration — holding keeps ~${{ '%.2f'|format(home_value/1e6) }}M of leveraged net worth in one house, whereas selling and investing spreads it out. See docs/sf-rental-tax-reference.md for the cited tax/eviction details.
