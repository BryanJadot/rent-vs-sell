The rental-tax machinery roughly washes out for you. While renting, the property runs a tax loss each year (rent minus operating costs, mortgage interest, and ~${{ '{:,.0f}'.format(annual_depreciation) }}/yr depreciation). Because your income is >$150k those losses are suspended and released at sale, deducted at your {{ '%.0f'|format(marginal_tax*100) }}% rate (+${{ '{:,.0f}'.format(we.deprec_release) }}). But the IRS then recaptures the depreciation at {{ '%.1f'|format(deprec_recapture_rate*100) }}% (fed 25% + CA ordinary) (−${{ '{:,.0f}'.format(we.recapture) }}). Net of the two: {{ deprec_net }} — essentially a wash, because California taxes recapture as ordinary income at nearly the same rate you deduct at. None of it helps monthly cash flow; it only lands at sale. This interaction is genuinely complex — confirm with a CPA.
